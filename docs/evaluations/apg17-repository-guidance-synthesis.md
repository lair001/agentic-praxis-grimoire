# APG17 Repository-Guidance Synthesis

## Objective and accepted owner

APG17 accepts ADR 0013 and implements one provisional
`synthesizing-repository-guidance` leaf. The skill owns decomposition and
bounded ownership, provenance, privacy, migration, rejection, rollback, and
source-preservation dispositions for mixed guidance before any rewrite. Its
conceptual boundary is **classify and disposition; do not rewrite or migrate**.

The retained capability is narrower than a writing skill, standards catalog,
migration engine, or root-modularization command. It grants no implementation,
cutover, deletion, publication, maturity, or successor-phase authority.

The maintainer confirmed that both same-name workflow routers were visible and

supplied result as the subsequent external disposition that closes APG16's

## Native authoring and APG lifecycle boundary

Current official Codex skill documentation and the available native technical
authoring capability were compared with APG's authoring guide, project model,
and provenance policy.

- Native Codex skill authoring owns technically valid skill-file construction
  after an owner, trigger, scenario contract, and destination are accepted.
- APG's authoring lifecycle owns new-capability adoption, behavior-bearing
  correction, evidence, review, validation, maturity, and removal.
- Repository-guidance synthesis is a prerequisite only when a mixed corpus
  still needs unit, owner, rights, privacy, or migration dispositions.
- Known skill authoring, simple documentation correction, and accepted
  implementation or debugging work are synthesis non-triggers.
- No owner automatically authorizes migration, deletion, cutover, publication,
  destructive action, or maturity.

A separate public skill-writing leaf is not justified.

## Source, rights, and privacy model

The source analysis used bounded maintainer-authored private guidance, one
general engineering source, a representative historic manager-prompt corpus,
three representative private skills, current official Codex guidance, native
authoring capability, and APG's current owners. Public expression is
independently written. Private ownership does not establish publication
eligibility, and source packaging does not establish a public capability.

Raw medical, cognitive, diagnostic, accessibility, deeply personal,
productivity, machine-specific, and private-integration detail was neither
retained nor published. `keep-private` is a valid terminal result. Missing
source identity or reuse rights blocks copied or adapted work; bounded
independent analysis may continue only where allowed.

## Frozen scenarios and baseline

The sixteen-family contract was frozen after four read-only source and baseline
reviews and before candidate wording:

- positive mixed-root, private-skill, external-methodology, historic-prompt,
  and root-modularization cases;
- known-authoring, simple-documentation, and direct-implementation
  non-triggers;
- sensitive-guidance, conflicting-authority, missing-rights, and mixed-unit
  edges;
- one-to-one and rewrite-first fault cases; and
- existing-owner correction and future-profile maintenance cases.

Ordinary reasoning reached correct owners and safe dispositions for all
sixteen. Its material limitation was not correctness or table formatting; it
was lower repeatability for coherent splitting, coverage, stable records,
migration dependencies, rollback, and explicit source preservation in the
mixed cases. This is baseline and retention evidence, not a claim of
comparative superiority.

Two fresh evaluators applied disjoint A-H and I-P halves to the authored
candidate. All positives produced complete dispositions, every non-trigger
remained a non-trigger, rights and authority faults stopped safely, personal
and machine guidance remained private, rewrite and mirroring demands were
rejected, and sources remained unchanged. Candidate correction count: zero.
