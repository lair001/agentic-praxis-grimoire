# APG10 Karpathy Guidelines Evaluation

## Objective and outcome

APG10 evaluates one experimental coding-guideline source against APG's six
current owners, freezes observable scenarios before any correction, adopts only
a demonstrated gap through independent APG-native synthesis, and records the
remaining dispositions.

Outcome: one targeted correction to the existing implementation-discipline
leaf. No seventh skill, universal coding manifesto, support directory,
dependency, or maturity change is introduced.

## Source class and provenance

The source is public external evidence, captured separately as experimental

[`64723a49ea6117894304eb491f0d32a60570bf45`](https://github.com/multica-ai/andrej-karpathy-skills/commit/64723a49ea6117894304eb491f0d32a60570bf45).
The leaf and README declare MIT, but that tree supplies no complete license
text, copyright notice, or notice file. Its linked public observation was not
readable in full during APG10.

Copied and adapted expression remain blocked. APG10 uses independently written
`synthesized` expression for the one adopted correction and records the
source's influence. No third-party notice is added because no copied or adapted
material entered APG and no complete notice payload was available.

## Frozen scenario contract

Before any skill change, APG10 fixed twelve public-safe scenarios: two
independent positives, one non-trigger, and one edge for each family. Fresh
read-only workers received clean context, current owners, and only their frozen
family. They did not inspect the external source, source-derived proposed
wording, candidate text, or one another's results.

Observable dimensions covered assumptions, alternatives, semantic
traceability, speculative abstraction, current-change cleanup, authority and
project policy, verifiable acceptance, proportionality, and escalation. A gap
required two independent materially partial positives, one high-risk weakness,
or repeated real-project omission evidence. Wording preference alone was
insufficient.

## Baseline findings

### Assumptions and alternatives

Both positives passed. The design leaf made compatibility, ownership, and
privacy assumptions visible; compared credible alternatives and the status quo;
preserved reserved decisions; and defined acceptance and implementation
boundaries. Straightforward accepted implementation remained a non-trigger.
Low-risk reversible documentation uncertainty could proceed under an explicit
bounded convention and checks without mandatory clarification.

Disposition: no material gap.

### Traceability and speculative scope

Both positives passed. Current owners excluded a plugin framework,
configurability, a generic normalization pipeline, and unrelated refactors.
The non-trigger retained tests, documentation, generated examples, and
consistency surfaces required by accepted behavior. The edge retained a fixture
and compatibility note serving a preserved contract and verification rather
than a literal feature line.

Disposition: no material gap.

### Current-change cleanup

The non-trigger already excluded unrelated pre-existing dead code and
formatting. The edge already preserved generated, migration, compatibility, or
externally owned artifacts when project policy or removal authority was absent.

Both positives were materially partial. Passing behavior and evidence could
coexist with a locally owned import and helper, or a fixture and setup branch,
whose only purpose ended because of the authorized change. The existing
procedure permitted but did not require their disposition. This met the frozen
two-independent-scenario threshold.

## Adopted owner and behavior

Only `skills/implementing-with-test-discipline/SKILL.md` changes. Its narrow-
implementation step now includes locally owned code or test artifacts whose
sole purpose ended because of the authorized change, conditional on clear
repository policy and removal authority. The same step continues to keep
unrelated cleanup and new abstractions outside scope.

The root instructions and other five leaves remain unchanged. Families A and B
did not demonstrate a gap; review already checks scope and the complete change;
debugging had no unexplained failure to own; assignment composition was only
evaluation machinery.

## Candidate results

Fresh candidate application closed both current-change positives. The unrelated
cleanup non-trigger and project-owned lifecycle edge remained correct. All
applicable cleanup, traceability, authority, policy, acceptance,
proportionality, and escalation dimensions were adequate.

The candidate introduced no mandatory escalation for reversible uncertainty,
universal cleanup, fixed workflow chain, fixed question cadence, universal
plan, universal test sequence, line threshold, abstraction threshold, or
project-policy import. A separate non-author review accepted the complete leaf
with no blocker, material finding, minor finding, or second correction need.

## Authority, privacy, and proportionality

- The human task remains the only APG10 implementation, Git, push, and report
  authority.
- Source evidence, worker results, and passing scenarios do not expand that
  authority.
- The correction applies only to locally owned artifacts and preserves target-
  project lifecycle and removal decisions.
- Publication-safe files contain no private checkout, source topology, local
  path, report identity, or private worker result.
- Low-risk reversible uncertainty remains proportionate rather than a mandatory
  stop.
- The correction count is one for one leaf; no second correction was needed.

## Maturity and skill count

APG retains six direct-child skills and six matching Codex discovery links. No
support directory or seventh candidate exists. All six skills remain
`provisional`; APG10 scenario evidence does not satisfy or replace APG13's
individual maturity review.

## Independent review

The concealed-source baseline used one fresh worker per family. A fresh
candidate evaluator applied the changed leaf to the unchanged current-change
scenarios. A separate fresh non-author reviewed the complete resulting leaf.
The final integrated diff receives a separate fresh review after all durable
records and validation are present.

## Limitations

- The scenario results are deliberate textual applications, not automatic-
  trigger telemetry or controlled comparative evidence.
- The scenarios supply no executable implementation fixture.
- APG10 does not establish the external source's complete license and notice
  chain or authorize copied or adapted use.
- Public v0.1.0, public-backed global integration, RepoMap, and Superpowers
  retirement remain unchanged.
- APG10 does not promote a skill, validate a release, or begin APG11.
