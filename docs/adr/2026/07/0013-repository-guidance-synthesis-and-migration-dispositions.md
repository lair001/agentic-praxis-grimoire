# ADR 0013: Repository-Guidance Synthesis and Migration Dispositions

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-20

## Acceptance authority

The human maintainer's APG17 assignment accepts this decision because the
candidate met the frozen-scenario, bounded-dogfood, privacy, ownership, and
review retention threshold. Acceptance authorizes the APG17 development
artifacts only. It does not authorize source migration, root reduction, private
skill removal, a language profile, manager-prompt capability, publication,
maturity promotion, or APG18.

## Context

Repository and private instruction corpora often combine universal authority,
reusable procedure, language guidance, exact project commands, machine state,
personal preferences, externally sourced material, and stale rules. Neither a
source file nor a private skill boundary establishes a coherent public
capability. Rewriting first risks weakening authority, copying expression
without established rights, publishing private context, duplicating an
existing owner, and removing the only current source before discovery and
rollback are proven.

Ordinary reasoning correctly classified all sixteen APG17 scenario families,
but was less repeatable for mixed-unit splitting, coverage accounting,
migration dependencies, rollback, and explicit source preservation. Native
Codex skill-authoring guidance constructs a technically valid skill after an
owner, trigger, scenario contract, and destination are accepted; it does not
own the preceding corpus-classification decision.

APG17 therefore evaluates one bounded owner for this pre-rewrite decision.

## Decision

### Normative ownership

`synthesizing-repository-guidance` owns classification and disposition of a
dense, duplicated, mixed-scope, private, or source-derived guidance corpus
before rewrite or migration. Its conceptual boundary is:

```text
classify and disposition; do not rewrite or migrate
```

Related owners remain distinct:

- the project model owns artifact destinations and the new-capability
  threshold;
- the provenance policy owns source identity, derivation, reuse rights, and
  required notices;
- the APG authoring guide owns accepted skill creation and behavior-bearing
  correction;
- native Codex skill-authoring guidance owns technical skill-file construction
  after the semantic contract is accepted;
- repository policy owns exact commands, versions, frameworks, test gates,
  migration, deployment, release, destructive action, and cutover; and
- private overlays own machine, personal, sensitive, and private-repository
  guidance.

The synthesis skill proposes owners and prerequisites. It does not replace any
of these owners or expand their authority.

### Disposition map

The skill splits mixed content into coherent units and records, for each unit:

- a stable local identifier, source class, problem, and applicability;
- current and proposed owner;
- source and rights status;
- authority, precedence, conflict, and overlap;
- one bounded disposition;
- acceptance evidence and migration dependency;
- rollback or restoration boundary; and
- public/private treatment.

The accepted vocabulary is:

```text
retain-root
route-existing-owner
synthesize-existing-owner-correction
candidate-new-skill
candidate-language-profile
core-documentation
deterministic-tool-candidate
project-owned
keep-private
evidence-only
defer
reject
split-required
blocked-source-or-rights
```

A Markdown table plus bounded detail is sufficient. APG does not require JSON,
a registry, a database, or a universal migration schema.

### No one-to-one migration

Source files, private skill directories, and repeated prose do not establish
public capability boundaries. Repetition is evidence to inspect, not adoption
proof. Existing owners are preferred, and a new skill must satisfy the accepted
new-capability threshold. Mixed units are split before owner selection.

Every source instruction remains in place until a later, separately authorized
migration supplies shadow behavior, discovery, override, cutover, rollback,
and restoration evidence. A disposition is not implementation authority.

### Privacy and sensitive guidance

Public APG receives no medical, cognitive, diagnostic, deeply personal,
accessibility, productivity, machine-specific, or private-integration detail.
Raw sensitive guidance is not retained even in APG's publication-excluded
evidence. `keep-private` is a valid terminal disposition.

A reusable public principle motivated by private context requires independent
engineering evidence and independently written expression that does not depend
on or reveal the private detail.

### Source-derived work

Source identity, derivation, reuse eligibility, and required notices are
established before copied or adapted work. Missing identity or rights blocks
that work. Bounded independently written analysis from observable facts may
continue only when allowed, and no actor invents provenance or permission.

Source packaging and authoritative tone do not create an APG owner. Existing
owners or independently written synthesis are preferred.

### Output and authority

The synthesis skill produces a reviewable disposition map and public-safe
aggregate. It does not author the final skill, root file, language profile,
policy, tool, or migration. It does not rewrite, remove, publish, decommission,
cut over, or authorize a candidate. A later accepted phase owns each proposed
implementation or migration.

### Relationship to the workflow router

The APG workflow router may route a mixed-guidance ownership or migration-plan
case to `synthesizing-repository-guidance`. The synthesis leaf does not route
ordinary repository work, duplicate router behavior, or become a mandatory
session entry point. Known skill authoring, simple documentation edits, and
accepted implementation or debugging tasks remain synthesis non-triggers.

### Relationship to future manager-prompt work

The historic manager-prompt sample shows a plausible distinct boundary for
constructing a top-level Codex assignment from an already human-approved
roadmap. APG17 records that as a future capability candidate only. It creates
no skill, final name, prompt, or implementation and does not preauthorize later
work. Human-approved roadmap authority remains external.

## Alternatives considered

### Continue ad hoc classification

Viable for simple or one-off cases. Rejected as the only owner for mixed
corpora because the baseline showed inconsistent coverage, migration,
restoration, and source-preservation fields across the materially complex
families.

### Create one public skill per private source artifact

Rejected. File and private-skill boundaries mix capabilities, project policy,
machine state, preferences, and duplicate existing procedure.

### Use the native skill writer as the classification owner

Rejected. Native technical authoring applies after the semantic owner and
contract are accepted. Moving it earlier confuses valid file construction with
capability adoption.

### Add a broad public coding-standards skill

Rejected. A broad standards owner would combine unrelated triggers, duplicate
repository authority and existing leaves, and encourage private or
language-specific detail to become universal procedure.

### Create a generalized migration engine

Rejected. APG17 needs a bounded decision record, not runtime, registry, state,
automatic rewrite, or destructive cutover machinery.

### Use one bounded disposition skill

Accepted. One narrow pre-rewrite procedure supplies stable unit splitting,
ownership, rights, privacy, migration, rollback, and source-preservation fields
while leaving implementation with current owners.

### Keep all private guidance private

Safe and valid for personal, machine, sensitive, and private-repository units.
Rejected as a universal disposition because some independently evidenced
reusable behavior already has public owners or may justify a future capability.

### Selectively synthesize only reusable capability gaps

Accepted as the migration policy. Existing owners remain preferred; only
coherent uncovered problems enter the separately governed new-skill lifecycle.

## Consequences

- Development gains one `provisional` synthesis leaf and one additional
  routable capability-map entry.
- The six stable leaves and provisional router remain unchanged.
- Public v0.2.0 and the project/user/release six-skill lifecycle contracts are
  unchanged.
- Mixed guidance work receives an auditable map without implying rewrite or
  cutover.
- Private, sensitive, machine, and project-specific guidance can terminate at
  `keep-private` or `project-owned` without a public counterpart.
- The disposition vocabulary increases consistency but does not replace
  judgment, provenance review, architecture acceptance, or legal advice.
- The catalog grows to eight development skills, increasing discovery and
  maintenance cost that later readiness and release phases must evaluate.

## Migration and rollback

APG17 performs no guidance migration. A future migration must accept exact
source scope, replacement owners, shadow behavior, discovery, overrides,
cutover evidence, and byte-restorable rollback before removing any source.

If the provisional synthesis leaf becomes unsound, remove its canonical leaf,
checked projection, catalog row, capability-map entry, and focused scenario
contract. Restore the preceding current-development known-unmanaged set and
every current-state catalog count, project-integration description, and
distribution-compatibility statement to the prior seven-skill development
shape. Preserve this ADR, evaluation, exit, and their historical outcome as
evidence. Source guidance requires no restoration because APG17 does not change
it.

## Deferred decisions

- any root-guidance rewrite, reduction, migration, or cutover;
- private skill correction, decommission, removal, or public replacement;
- a manager-to-top-level prompt-construction skill;
- acceptance or implementation of ADR 0012 or any language profile;
- synthesis maturity beyond `provisional`;
- project/user distribution or public-release inclusion;
- v0.3 publication; and
- APG18 or any later roadmap phase.
