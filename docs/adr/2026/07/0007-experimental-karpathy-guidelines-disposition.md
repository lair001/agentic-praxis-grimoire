# ADR 0007: Experimental Karpathy Guidelines Disposition

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-19

## Acceptance authority

The human maintainer's APG10 assignment accepts APG9 and authorizes this bounded
source evaluation, one evidence-supported correction to an existing owner,
private development commit and push, and managed reports. It does not authorize
a seventh skill, maturity promotion, public or reference mutation, global
integration change, Superpowers restoration, release publication, or APG11.

## Context

APG9 closed the v0.1 epic, accepted ADR 0006, retained six `provisional`
skills, and identified three hypotheses from an experimental coding-guideline
source. APG10 must establish provenance, test current APG behavior before
changing an owner, and disposition every material source idea without treating
source packaging or familiarity as adoption evidence.

The captured source is byte-identical to
[`multica-ai/andrej-karpathy-skills`](https://github.com/multica-ai/andrej-karpathy-skills)
at immutable commit
[`64723a49ea6117894304eb491f0d32a60570bf45`](https://github.com/multica-ai/andrej-karpathy-skills/commit/64723a49ea6117894304eb491f0d32a60570bf45).
Its leaf and README declare MIT, but that revision contains no full license
text, copyright notice, or notice file. The source links an Andrej Karpathy
public observation, but its complete body and reuse terms were not independently
available during APG10. Copied or adapted use therefore remains blocked.

## Decision

### Evaluation method

APG10 froze twelve public-safe scenarios before any candidate wording: two
positive cases, one non-trigger, and one edge for each of three families.
Fresh concealed-source workers applied only current APG instructions and owners.
A material gap required two independent materially partial positives, one high-
risk weakness, or repeated real-project omission evidence.

Families A and B had no material gap. Current design behavior surfaced
consequential assumptions and compared credible alternatives while keeping
straightforward and low-risk reversible work proportional. Current planning,
implementation, and review behavior traced accepted work semantically, rejected
speculative frameworks and adjacent refactors, and retained necessary contract,
consistency, documentation, and evidence surfaces.

Family C met the threshold. In two independent cases, current implementation
text permitted locally owned implementation or test artifacts made unnecessary
solely by the authorized change to remain while behavior and tests still passed.
The same current text correctly excluded unrelated pre-existing cleanup and
preserved generated, migration, compatibility, and external lifecycle policy.

### Adopted correction

APG10 makes one coherent correction to
`implementing-with-test-discipline`. Its narrow-implementation step now includes
locally owned code or test artifacts whose only purpose ended because of the
authorized change, but only when repository policy and removal authority are
clear. Unrelated cleanup and speculative abstraction remain outside scope.

Fresh application to the frozen Family C cases made both positives adequate,
preserved the non-trigger and edge, and introduced no universal cleanup mandate,
new authority, fixed workflow, test-order rule, or mandatory stop for low-risk
reversible progress. A separate non-author leaf review accepted the resulting
leaf without finding or second-correction need.

### Derivation and notice

The correction is independently written APG-native `synthesized` expression
based on current ownership, the frozen scenario gap, and repository boundaries.
APG10 copies or adapts no source heading, slogan, example, heuristic, template,
sequence, or recognizable structure. The source materially influenced the
evaluation, so public provenance records that influence and the unresolved
reuse boundary. No third-party notice is added because no copied or adapted
material entered APG and the source supplies no complete notice payload to
preserve.

### Existing ownership and skill count

No root instruction, design, planning, review, debugging, or bounded-assignment
procedure changes. Their frozen cases either passed or did not own the
demonstrated gap. The implementation leaf remains the existing owner. APG
retains exactly six direct-child skills and six matching discovery projections;
all six remain `provisional`.

## Alternatives considered

### Add the source as a seventh skill

Rejected. Its themes overlap current owners, its absolute rules conflict with
proportionality or project-owned policy, and its reuse boundary is incomplete.

### Add cross-cutting root rules for assumptions or traceability

Rejected. Families A and B passed under current owners. Root restatement would
duplicate trigger-specific procedure without a demonstrated universal gap.

### Make no skill change

Rejected after baseline. Family C met the two-independent-scenario material-gap
threshold, and one existing owner could correct it without over-triggering.

### Add a general cleanup rule or deterministic validator

Rejected. Cleanup requires ownership and policy judgment, and the demonstrated
gap is narrower than a repository-wide mandate or stable mechanical invariant.

### Copy or adapt the external wording under its MIT declaration

Rejected. A label and README assertion are not a complete license and notice
payload, and independently written synthesis is sufficient.

## Consequences

- APG records one source-influenced, scenario-supported implementation
  correction without introducing another skill or universal workflow.
- Cleanup required for coherent completion is distinguishable from unrelated
  pre-existing cleanup and project-owned lifecycle decisions.
- Families A and B remain evidence-backed `already-covered` dispositions rather
  than duplicated policy.
- Copied and adapted use of the source remains unauthorized unless a future
  phase resolves the complete permission and notice chain.
- APG10 evidence is bounded textual evaluation, not automatic-trigger,
  comparative-superiority, or stable-maturity evidence.

## Rollback

If later evidence shows over-triggering or policy drift, revert the one
implementation-procedure sentence, restore the baseline leaf hash, rerun the
Family C scenarios and complete repository gates, and supersede this decision
without deleting its evidence. The six projections require no separate content
rollback because they resolve to the canonical leaves.

## Non-goals

APG10 does not promote a skill, create a support directory or evaluator, change
tests or dependencies, define universal style or error-handling policy, modify
public or reference repositories, alter global integration or RepoMap, restore
Superpowers, publish v0.2, or begin APG11.

## Deferred decisions

- Any future copied or adapted use requires a new source-rights and notice
  review.
- Automatic-trigger or controlled comparative evaluation remains subject to
  the existing isolation conditions.
- Skill maturity remains an individual APG13 decision.
- APG11 remains a separately authorized phase.
