## Calibration-first and no-leaf boundary

An evaluator independent of candidate authorship freezes the environment,
behavior families, calibration and sealed inputs, reserve cases, rubric,
reviewer contract, sample bounds, execution subset, and baseline-adequacy rule
before authoring. Calibration runs without the candidate. A lone outlier,
verbosity difference, or minor-correction label does not establish a material
deficit.

When ordinary Codex meets the frozen adequacy rule, APG3 authors no skill leaf
and closes with a truthful rejection or deferral disposition. That no-leaf
outcome is a successful APG3 result.

## Confirmation and correction boundary

A permitted candidate may receive at most one bounded correction from
calibration evidence, followed by a complete calibration rerun. The frozen
candidate then enters sealed confirmation. The author cannot inspect sealed or
reserve inputs, prompts, or expected labels before freezing. Two independently
contextualized non-author reviewers assess randomized, concealed-condition
outputs under the frozen scoring and disagreement rules.

Confirmation failure cannot trigger another correction. A tie, no clear
advantage, unresolved disagreement after the bounded reserve expansion, second
correction need, new problem class, or required rubric change ends APG3 without
adoption.

## Downstream-execution evidence boundary

Any downstream-compliance advantage requires actual matched fresh-worker
execution in synthetic, public-safe, disposable or read-only environments that
begin from identical snapshots. The preselected subset includes read-only,
isolated-write, and blocked-or-needs-context cases. A disjoint parallel case is
added only when APG3 intends to claim parallel-ownership behavior.

Only bounded observables are recorded. Reviewer prediction is not execution
evidence. When safe execution is unavailable, downstream compliance is `not
observed` and cannot count as an advantage. All claims remain sample-specific.
